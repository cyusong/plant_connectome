In plants under hypersaline stress, the main transporter that extrudes sodium ions (Na <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> ) is the Na <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> /H <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> antiporter SOS1. Different from land plants, the intertidal macroalgae, <i>Neopyropia/Neoporphyra</i> contains an animal-type Na <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> /K <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> -ATPase as well as the SOS1 system. However, the contribution of Na <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> /K <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> -ATPase to the K <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> /Na <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> homeostasis of intertidal macroalgae remains unclear. In this study, we analyzed the function of Na <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> /K <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> -ATPase in the response of <i>Neoporphyra haitanensis</i> to salt stress from the perspective of ion transport dynamics. Both the transcript level of <i>NhNKA2</i> and enzyme activity of Na <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> /K <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> -ATPase increased in the early response of <i>N. haitanensis</i> thalli to hypersaline stress. Addition of ouabain, an inhibitor of Na <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> /K <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> -ATPase, resulted in Na <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> accumulation in the cells, severe K <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> leakage from the thalli, and then remarkably disturbed the K <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> /Na <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> homeostasis in <i>N. haitanensis</i> thalli. This disruption might induce a significant decrease in photosynthesis and a severe oxidative damage in thalli. Accordingly, these results suggested that the important role of Na <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> /K <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> -ATPase in the resistance of intertidal macroalgae to hypersaline stress, and shed light on the diversity of K <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> /Na <sup><sup>+</sup></sup> homeostasis maintenance mechanisms in plants.
