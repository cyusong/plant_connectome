The concentration of CO<sub>2</sub> in the atmosphere has increased over the past 200 years and is expected to continue rising in the next 50 years at a rate of 3 ppm·year<sup>-1</sup>. Since anion channels are also permeable to <math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><mrow><mtext>N</mtext> <msubsup><mtext>O</mtext> <mn>3</mn> <mo>-</mo></msubsup> </mrow> </math> we hypothesize that high <math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><mrow><mtext>HC</mtext> <msubsup><mtext>O</mtext> <mn>3</mn> <mo>-</mo></msubsup> </mrow> </math> , or even CO<sub>2</sub>, would also promote a decrease of cytosolic <math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><mrow><mtext>N</mtext> <msubsup><mtext>O</mtext> <mn>3</mn> <mo>-</mo></msubsup> </mrow> </math> ( <math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><mrow><mtext>N</mtext> <msubsup><mtext>O</mtext> <mn>3</mn> <mo>-</mo></msubsup> <msub><mrow></mrow> <mtext>c</mtext></msub> </mrow> </math> ). In this work we have used <math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><mrow><mtext>N</mtext> <msubsup><mtext>O</mtext> <mn>3</mn> <mo>-</mo></msubsup> </mrow> </math> - and Cl<sup>-</sup>-selective microelectrodes for the continuous monitoring of the cytosolic concentration of both anions in <i>P. oceanica</i> leaf cells.
